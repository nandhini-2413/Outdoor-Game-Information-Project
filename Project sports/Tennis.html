Tennis – A Sport of Skill and Endurance

What is Tennis?

Tennis is a fast-paced racket sport played between two (singles) or four (doubles) players. The aim is to hit the ball over the net into the opponent’s court in a way they can't return it.

Matches are played on grass, clay, or hard courts and are divided into sets and games. Players need strength, precision, and agility to win points and outplay their opponents.

Key Features

  • Played with rackets and a felt-covered rubber ball
  • Singles (1v1) or Doubles (2v2)
  • Requires speed, technique, and endurance
  • Scoring: Points, Games, Sets
  • Global tournaments: Wimbledon, US Open, Australian Open, French Open

History & Growth

Tennis evolved from a medieval French game and was formalized in the late 19th century. It is now one of the most watched and played sports worldwide, with a strong international professional circuit.

Why Tennis Stands Out

  • Improves reflexes, fitness, and mental focus
  • Individual and doubles gameplay options
  • Played both recreationally and professionally
  • Can be played on various surfaces year-round

Short Version

Tennis is a competitive racket sport played on various surfaces. It builds stamina, coordination, and mental sharpness while offering individual glory and global fanfare.
